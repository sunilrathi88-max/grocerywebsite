## 5 Simple Home Tests for Purity

1. **The Water Float Test:** Drop a teaspoon of lal mirch powder into a glass of water. Pure powder floats for a few seconds then settles slowly. Adulterants like brick powder or sand will sink immediately, while sawdust will continue to float.
2. **Artificial Colour Test:** Sprinkle a small amount of powder on the surface of water. If streaks of vibrant red or orange descend immediately, artificial water-soluble dyes (like Sudan dye) are likely present. Pure spice releases colour slowly and naturally.
3. **The Texture Rub Test:** Rub a small amount of powder between your fingers. If it feels gritty or sandy, brick powder or talc is present. Pure cold-ground lal mirch should feel smooth and slightly oily due to preserved natural fats.
4. **Starch/Filler Test (Iodine Test):** Add a few drops of iodine solution to a mixture of powder and water. If it turns blue, starch (from rice or potato flour) has been added as a filler.
5. **The Aroma Test:** Pure, cold-ground red chilli has a sharp, characteristic pungent aroma that hits the back of the throat. Adulterated powder either smells flat/earthy or has a chemical, 'off' scent.

> [!TIP]
> **The Oily Touch**
> Pure red chilli is naturally high in oils (capsaicinoids). When you rub it between your fingers, it should feel slightly "soft" or oily, not dry or scratchy. If it feels like sand, that's a sign of brick powder. If it's too bright and stains your skin instantly, artificial dyes might be present.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do I know if my lal mirch powder is pure?**
A: Perform the water float test and the rub test described above. Quality powder floats uniformly and feels smooth.

**Q: Is "bright red" chilli powder always better?**
A: No. Extremely bright or "neon" red often indicates artificial colouring. Natural Mathania chilli is vibrant red, but generic powders that look too bright should be tested.

**Q: What is the best way to store red chilli powder?**
A: In an airtight dark glass jar in a cool, dry place. Avoid plastic containers which can absorb oils or lead to faster oxidation.
