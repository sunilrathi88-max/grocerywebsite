# Dhaniya Pani: Simple Coriander Detox Water Indians Have Used for Centuries

Dhaniya pani — coriander seed water — is one of Ayurveda's simplest and most validated functional beverages. Overnight soaked coriander seeds produce a cooling, digestive, anti-inflammatory water that addresses multiple modern health concerns simultaneously.

## The Ayurvedic Profile: Cooling and Digestive

In Ayurveda, coriander is known as _Kusthumbari_. It is one of the few spices that is **Tri-doshic** (balancing for Vata, Pitta, and Kapha), though its primary action is 'Sheetal' (cooling).

What makes it effective?

- **Digestive Stimulant**: It kindles _Agni_ (digestive fire) without increasing _Pitta_ (heat).
- **Anti-Inflammatory**: High concentrations of linalool and geraniol act as natural soothing agents for the gut lining.
- **Detoxifying**: It is a mild diuretic, helping the kidneys flush excess salts and water-borne toxins.

## How to Make Authentic Dhaniya Pani

**The standard mistake**: Using boiling water. High heat destroys the delicate aromatic oils that provide the therapeutic benefit.

**The correct method (Cold-Infusion)**:

1. Cracker one teaspoon of [Rathi Naturals Whole Coriander Seeds](/products/coriander-powder) (or half a teaspoon of powder) slightly.
2. Add to 1 cup of room-temperature water.
3. Let it soak overnight (7-8 hours).
4. Strain and drink on an empty stomach first thing in the morning.
