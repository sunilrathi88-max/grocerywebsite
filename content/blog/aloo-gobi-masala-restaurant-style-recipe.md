## Recipe Deep-Dive: Technique, Ratios and Common Mistakes

The specific ratios and technique for this recipe have been developed through decades of Indian cooking tradition. The standard approach — adding garam masala for curry at the wrong phase of cooking — is the most common error that prevents home cooks from achieving professional results.

The correct method positions garam masala for curry at the phase in the recipe where its aromatic compounds can bloom without being destroyed: in hot fat at the beginning for foundational flavour, or in the last minutes of cooking for aromatic finishing. Never add cold-ground garam masala powder to boiling water directly without a fat phase intermediary.

> [!TIP]
> **The Finishing Dust**
> For dry vegetable dishes (sabzis) like Aloo Gobi, wait until the very end to add the Garam Masala. Sprinkle it over the top just before you turn off the heat. This "finishing dust" method preserves the brightest, most floral notes of the spice blend.
