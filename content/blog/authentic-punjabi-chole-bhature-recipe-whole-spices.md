## Recipe Deep-Dive: Technique, Ratios and Common Mistakes

The specific ratios and technique for this recipe have been developed through decades of Indian cooking tradition. The standard approach — adding garam masala powder at the wrong phase of cooking — is the most common error that prevents home cooks from achieving professional results.

The correct method positions garam masala powder at the phase in the recipe where its aromatic compounds can bloom without being destroyed: in hot fat at the beginning for foundational flavour, or in the last minutes of cooking for aromatic finishing. Never add cold-ground garam masala powder to boiling water directly without a fat phase intermediary.

> [!TIP]
> **The Bhatura Balance**
> A perfect bhatura is light and airy, while the chole is heavy and spicy. To balance this, we bloom our Garam Masala in ghee at the very start of the masala-making process. This ensures the spices are "cooked through" and won't feel raw against the delicate bread.
