# Dhaniya Gosht: Rustic Rajasthani Lamb Curry Loaded with Coriander

Dhaniya gosht — coriander lamb curry — uses dhaniya powder at nearly 4 tablespoons per 500g of meat. This is not a background spice in this dish. Coriander is the hero, the base, and the finish simultaneously. It is Rajasthan's most assertive lamb curry, and it is extraordinary.

## The Essential Technique: Fat-Mediated Blooming

Understanding the core technique for this preparation transforms results dramatically. In Dhaniya Gosht, the powder is added in three distinct phases to build layers of flavor.

1. **Foundational Layer**: Sautéed with onions and ginger-garlic to create a thick, earthy base.
2. **Body Layer**: Cooked with the meat during the 'Bhuna' phase to infuse the lamb fibers.
3. **Aromatic Finish**: A final pinch added just before the lid is placed for the final slow-simmer.

By using [Rathi Naturals' cold-ground dhaniya powder](/products/coriander-powder), you ensure that even the foundational layer retains its character, while the finish provides a citrusy lift that compensates for the richness of the lamb fat.

## Recipe Deep-Dive: The Slow-Simmer Method

**The standard mistake**: Adding dhaniya powder to a watery curry at the end. This results in a 'gritty' feel and a raw spice taste. Coriander is a thickening agent as well as a flavoring; it requires 'blooming' in oil or ghee (BHUNA) to unlock its starches and oils.

**The Golden Rule**: Every spice works better when bloomed in hot fat first. The fat extracts the oil-soluble aromatic compounds (linalool), allowing them to distribute through the entire dish as the gravy forms.

> [!NOTE]
> **The Art of Blooming**
> "Blooming" your spices in hot fat is the secret to a professional-grade curry. The oil extracts the flavor compounds from the powder and distributes them evenly throughout the gravy. By adding the coriander in phases, you build a foundation of deep, earthy taste and a top layer of fresh aroma that makes the dish truly complex.

## Serving and Storage

Store your coriander powder in an airtight glass container away from heat and light. Quality [cold-ground coriander](/products/coriander-powder) retains full potency for 18–24 months when stored correctly. If your powder smells like dry soil with no citrus or floral note, it is past its best.

**FAQ**

- **Q: Why so much dhaniya powder?** It provides the texture and 'body' of the gravy, acting as a thickener without needing excessive onions.
- **Q: Can I use fresh coriander instead?** No, fresh leaves provide 'top notes' but not the earthy 'base notes' of the seed-based powder.
