## Recipe Deep-Dive: Technique, Ratios and Common Mistakes

The specific ratios and technique for this recipe have been developed through decades of Indian cooking tradition. The standard approach — adding red chilli powder at the wrong phase of cooking — is the most common error that prevents home cooks from achieving professional results.

The correct method positions red chilli powder at the phase in the recipe where its aromatic compounds can bloom without being destroyed: in hot fat at the beginning for foundational flavour, or in the last minutes of cooking for aromatic finishing. Never add cold-ground red chilli (lal mirch) powder to boiling water directly without a fat phase intermediary. The fat extracts the oil-soluble aromatic compounds first, then they distribute evenly through the entire dish as water is added. This principle applies across Indian cooking: every spice works better when bloomed in oil or ghee first.

> [!TIP]
> **The Ghee Secret**
> Whether you choose Mathania or Kashmiri, the secret to that restaurant-style "rogun" (red oil layer) is blooming. Because the pigments in these chillies are fat-soluble, adding them to hot ghee or oil for just 5 seconds before adding other ingredients will give you a color that's twice as intense.
