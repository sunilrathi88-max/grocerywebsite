# How to Make Dhaniya Powder at Home from Whole Coriander Seeds

Making dhaniya powder at home — dry roasting and grinding whole coriander seeds — produces a more aromatic powder than most commercially available alternatives. This guide covers the technique and explains why **cold-grinding** produces a fundamentally superior result to both home grinding and hot-ground commercial powder.

## The Home Method: Step-by-Step

1. **Clean**: Buy whole, bright green coriander seeds. Ensure they are free of stones or stems.
2. **Dry Roast (The Secret Step)**: Heat a heavy-bottomed pan. Add the seeds and roast on low heat for 2-3 minutes until they become fragrant and turn a shade darker. This 'unlocks' the internal oils.
3. **Cool**: Spread them on a plate and let them cool completely. Grinding warm seeds will lead to moisture and clumping.
4. **Grind**: Pulse in a high-speed blender for 10-15 seconds. Do not run the blender continuously, as the friction will create heat, destroying the volatile oils (Linalool).
5. **Store**: Sieve and store in a dark glass jar.

## Why Professional Cold-Grinding Wins

While the home method is better than 'cheap' store-bought powder, it suffers from one major flaw: **Blender Heat**. Even a 30-second pulse in a home mixer reaches temperatures of 50–60°C. At this temperature, the lightest, most citrusy aromatic notes of the coriander seed evaporate into your kitchen air instead of staying in the powder.
