## The Master Recipe: Bride-to-be Ubtan

**Ingredients (for full body application)**:

- 1/2 cup Chickpea Flour (Besan)
- 1 tablespoon Rathi Naturals Salem Turmeric Powder
- 2 tablespoons Sandalwood Powder
- 1 tablespoon Honey (optional - for dry skin)
- Milk or Rose Water (enough to make a thick, spreadable paste)

**Method**:

1. Mix all dry ingredients in a bowl.
2. Slowly add your liquid (milk for dry skin, rose water for oily skin) until you get a thick paste consistency.
3. Apply all over the body in circular motions.
4. Let it dry for 15–20 minutes until it starts to crack.
5. **The Secret Move**: Do not wash it off immediately. Instead, use your damp hands to 'rub' the dry paste off your skin in the opposite direction of hair growth. This helps remove fine holiday hair and deeply exfoliates.
6. Rinse with warm water. Do not use soap for at least 6 hours to let the turmeric do its work.

> [!NOTE]
> **The Exfoliation Technique**
> Rubbing the ubtan off in the direction opposite to hair growth helps remove fine hair and stimulates blood flow to the skin. This improves circulation and nutrient delivery, ensuring that your "wedding glow" isn't just surface-level but truly deep and long-lasting.

## How to Avoid the 'Yellow Bride' Nightmare

Most modern brides are terrified of turning yellow on their wedding day. Follow these three rules to ensure a glow, not a stain:

1. **Quality Matters**: Staining is often caused by 'Metanil Yellow' — a synthetic dye used in cheap turmeric. [Pure Salem Turmeric](/products/turmeric-powder) produces a warm, natural golden hue that washes off cleanly.
2. **The Fat Factor**: Always use full-fat milk or a teaspoon of coconut oil in your mixture. Curcumin prefers to bind with fats rather than your skin.
3. **The Post-Ubtan Wipe**: If any yellow tint remains, wipe the area with a cotton ball dipped in olive oil or almond oil. The colour will transfer to the oil immediately.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many days before the wedding should I start using Ubtan?**
A: We recommend starting 30 days before the wedding, using it once a week. This allows your skin to detoxify and build a natural luminosity without the risk of last-minute irritation.

**Q: Can Ubtan be used on children?**
A: Yes, traditional Indian 'Maalish' for babies often includes a very mild Ubtan. However, for children, use double the milk and only a small pinch of turmeric.

**Q: Does Ubtan expire?**
A: You can mix the dry ingredients and store them in a jar for 6 months. Only add the liquids (milk/rose water) right before you use it.
