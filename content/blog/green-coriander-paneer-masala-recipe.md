# Green Coriander Paneer Masala: Fresh Twist on a North Indian Classic

Hariyali paneer without spinach — using coriander as the sole green base — produces a more complex, less sweet version of the classic. The combination of fresh coriander leaves and [Dhaniya powder](/products/coriander-powder) creates a flavour depth that spinach-based versions cannot match.

## The Dual-Coriander Technique

Most "Hariyali" dishes rely on the bright, thin flavor of fresh herbs. While refreshing, this often lacks 'gravy body'. By adding [Rathi Naturals cold-ground coriander powder](/products/coriander-powder) to the green paste, we introduce:

1. **Texture**: The powder thickens the paste without needing excessive cream.
2. **Earthy Base**: The linalool in the seed-based powder provides a 'floor' for the fresh green notes of the leaf.
3. **Consistency**: It prevents the green sauce from leaching water on the plate.

## Preparation: The Hariyali Paste

1. Blanch a large bunch of fresh coriander for 30 seconds. Shock in ice water (this preserves the vibrant green).
2. Blend the leaves with 2 green chillies, 1 inch ginger, 4 garlic cloves, and **1 teaspoon of [Rathi Naturals Dhaniya Powder](/products/coriander-powder)**.
3. Sauté diced paneer in ghee until slightly golden. Remove.
4. In the same ghee, sauté the green paste on medium heat for 3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
5. Add 2 tablespoons of whisked curd or cream. Return the paneer, season with salt, and finish with a pinch of Garam Masala.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Designing the Depth**
> A true Hariyali dish needs more than just fresh leaves—it needs an anchor. By blending Rathi Naturals' cold-ground coriander powder into your green paste, you provide an earthy "floor" that lets the bright, citrusy notes of the fresh coriander shine. This combination ensures your gravy is thick, consistent, and full of flavor from the first bite to the last.

## Why Cold-Ground Matters for the Glow

A green curry is all about the visual. Conventional, hot-ground coriander powder often has a 'brownish' tinge due to over-grinding and oxidation. [Rathi Naturals' cold-ground dhaniya](/products/coriander-powder) is a bright, vibrant greenish-gold, ensuring your Hariyali gravy stays brilliantly green instead of turning muddy.

> [!NOTE]
> **The Science of Sizzle**
> Blooming the green paste in ghee is what transforms it from a "raw chutney" into a "cooked curry." The heat helps the flavors integrate and removes any harsh "grassy" notes. Because our dhaniya powder is cold-ground, it reacts beautifully to this quick sizzle, releasing its natural oils and creating a silky, aromatic sauce.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I add spinach to this?**
A: You can, but it will make the dish sweeter. This recipe aims for the 'bright and citrusy' profile that only pure coriander can deliver.

**Q: Why blanch the leaves?**
A: Blanching deactivates the enzymes that turn chlorophyll brown. If you skip this, your curry will turn into an olive-dark color within 20 minutes of cooking.
