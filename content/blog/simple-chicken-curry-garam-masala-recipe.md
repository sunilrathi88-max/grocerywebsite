## Recipe Deep-Dive: Technique, Ratios and Common Mistakes

The specific ratios and technique for this recipe have been developed through decades of Indian cooking tradition. The standard approach — adding garam masala for chicken curry at the wrong phase of cooking — is the most common error that prevents home cooks from achieving professional results.

The correct method positions garam masala for chicken curry at the phase in the recipe where its aromatic compounds can bloom without being destroyed: in hot fat at the beginning for foundational flavour, or in the last minutes of cooking for aromatic finishing. Never add cold-ground garam masala powder to boiling water directly without a fat phase intermediary.

> [!TIP]
> **The "Oil Release" Sign**
> When you see the oil starting to separate from your masala base, that's the time to add your Garam Masala. This ensures the spices fuse with the chicken fats right at the beginning, building a foundation of flavor that stays through the entire cooking process.
