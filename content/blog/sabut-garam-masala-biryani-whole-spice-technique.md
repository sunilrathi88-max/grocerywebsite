## Recipe Deep-Dive: Technique, Ratios and Common Mistakes

The specific ratios and technique for this recipe have been developed through decades of Indian cooking tradition. The standard approach — adding sabut garam masala at the wrong phase of cooking — is the most common error that prevents home cooks from achieving professional results.

The correct method positions sabut garam masala at the phase in the recipe where its aromatic compounds can bloom without being destroyed: in hot fat at the beginning for foundational flavour, or in the last minutes of cooking for aromatic finishing. Never add cold-ground garam masala powder to boiling water directly without a fat phase intermediary.

> [!TIP]
> **The "Sizzle and Pop" Rule**
> When using whole spices (Sabut Garam Masala), the oil should be hot enough to make them sizzle immediately but not so hot that they turn black. This brief 10-second window is where the magic happens. Match these whole spices with a pinch of our cold-ground Garam Masala for a flavor that is both deep and vibrant.
