## The 20-Minute Recipe: Simple Pili Moong Dal

**Ingredients (Serves 3–4)**:

- 1 cup Yellow Moong Dal (split yellow lentils)
- 3 cups Water
- 1/2 teaspoon Rathi Naturals Salem Turmeric Powder
- Salt to taste
- **For the Tadka (Tempering)**:
  - 1 tablespoon Ghee (or oil for vegan)
  - 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (Jeera)
  - 1 pinch Hing (Asafoetida)
  - 1–2 Dried Red Chillies
  - 1/2 teaspoon Rathi Naturals Salem Turmeric Powder (optional - for extra colour)

**Method**:

1. Wash the dal thoroughly until the water runs clear.
2. In a pressure cooker or heavy-bottomed pot, add dal, 3 cups of water, salt, and **1/2 teaspoon of Haldi**.
3. **If using a pressure cooker**: Cook for 2–3 whistles until the dal is soft and 'mushy'.
4. **If using a pot**: Bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 15–20 minutes until the dal is soft.
5. **The Tadka (The Magic Step)**: In a small frying pan, heat ghee until hot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add hing and red chillies. Turn off the heat.
6. **Pro-Tip**: Add a small pinch of haldi to the hot ghee for 2 seconds before pouring it over the dal. This 'blooms' the oils and gives that restaurant-style vibrant colour.
7. Pour the hot tadka over the dal, cover immediately for 1 minute to trap the aroma, and serve hot with steamed rice.

## 3 Quick Upgrades for Your Dal

- **The Rajasthani Style**: Add half a teaspoon of Amchur (Dry Mango Powder) to the dal after it's cooked for a tangy kick.
- **The Garlic Punch**: Add 4 crushed garlic cloves to the tadka along with the cumin seeds. Garlic and turmeric together are a powerful duo for cardiovascular health.
- **The 'Green' Dal**: Stir in a large handful of fresh chopped spinach (palak) 5 minutes before the dal is finished. The Vitamin K in spinach complements the anti-inflammatory action of the haldi.
