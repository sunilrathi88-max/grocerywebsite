# Aloo Haldi Sabzi: Simple Potato Curry with Only 4 Spices

The best aloo sabzi in India is also its simplest. Four spices: haldi, dhaniya, salt and hing — and a proper bhunao technique that creates restaurant-quality potato curry in 15 minutes flat.

## The 4-Spice Rule and Why It Works

In traditional Rajasthani cooking, we believe that less is often more. This sabzi relies on just four key spices: **Salem Turmeric**, **Ramganj Mandi Coriander**, Salt, and a pinch of **Hing**. When you use high-quality, cold-ground spices, you don't need a pantry full of ingredients to achieve depth of flavor. The turmeric provides the earthy base, the coriander adds a citrusy lift, and the hing provides that savory "umami" that ties it all together.

## Step-by-Step: Aloo Haldi Sabzi Recipe

1. **Prep the Potatoes**: Peel and dice 4 large potatoes into 1-inch cubes.
2. **The Tadka**: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a pan. Add a pinch of hing and a teaspoon of cumin seeds.
3. **Blooming the Spices**: Once the seeds splutter, add half a teaspoon of Rathi Naturals Haldi Powder and one teaspoon of Coriander Powder. Stir for just 5 seconds to avoid burning.
4. **The Bhunao**: Add the potatoes and salt. Stir well to coat every cube in the golden spice mix.
5. **Slow Cook**: Add a splash of water, cover, and cook on low heat until the potatoes are fork-tender (about 10-12 minutes).

> [!NOTE]
> **Flavor Extraction**
> Notice that we don't add red chilli powder in this version. The goal is to let the earthy, citrusy notes of the coriander and turmeric take center stage. This makes it a perfect, mild dish for children or as a soothing side for a spicy dal.

## Rajasthani Amchur Finishing Technique

To take this simple dish to the next level, we use the Rajasthani secret: **Amchur (Dried Mango Powder)**. Add half a teaspoon of amchur right at the end of cooking, after you've turned off the heat. This provides a sharp, tangy contrast to the earthy potatoes without the moisture of fresh lemon juice, keeping the potatoes perfectly crisp-tender.

> [!TIP]
> **The Final Touch**
> Garnish with a handful of freshly chopped coriander leaves. The green against the golden yellow makes for a stunning presentation, and the fresh herb aroma completes the sensory experience.

**Q: Can I use fresh turmeric instead of powder?**
A: Yes — grate fresh turmeric root at approximately 3:1 ratio (3 teaspoons fresh = 1 teaspoon powder). Fresh turmeric has higher moisture and slightly different flavour profile, somewhat brighter and less earthy than dried powder.

**Q: How long does haldi powder stay fresh?**
A: Properly stored (airtight dark container, below 25°C), quality cold-ground haldi powder retains full potency for 18–24 months. Hot-ground commercial powder typically loses significant aroma within 6–9 months of opening.
