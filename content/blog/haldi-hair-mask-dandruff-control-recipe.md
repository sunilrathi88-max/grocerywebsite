## The Scalp Detox Recipe: Haldi, Neem and Coconut

**Ingredients**:

- 1 tablespoon Rathi Naturals Salem Turmeric Powder
- 3 tablespoons Virgin Coconut Oil (carrier oil)
- 1 teaspoon Lemon Juice (to balance pH and dissolve flakes)
- Optional: 5 drops of Neem oil (for severe dandruff)

**Method**:

1. Warm the coconut oil slightly until it's liquid.
2. Mix in the haldi powder and lemon juice until you have a smooth, yellow oil.
3. **Application**: Partition your hair and apply the mixture specifically to the scalp using a cotton ball or your fingertips. Do not worry about the hair strands; the target is the skin.
4. Massage your scalp for 5 minutes to improve circulation.
5. Let it sit for 20–30 minutes. Use a shower cap to prevent staining your clothes.
6. Wash twice with a mild, sulphate-free shampoo. The yellow tint will wash out easily if you have used pure, high-quality turmeric.

> [!NOTE]
> **Synergy with Nature**
> This formula uses coconut oil to carry the turmeric's benefits deep into the scalp. Lemon juice helps balance the scalp's pH, while neem oil adds another layer of natural protection. Together, they create a potent "Scalp Detox" that clears flakes and supports overall hair health from the roots up.

## 3 Things to Know Before You Start

1. **Blonde/Light Hair Warning**: If you have very light blonde or bleached hair, turmeric _can_ stain the hair shaft. We recommend a patch test on a small strand of hair behind your ear first. For dark hair, there is zero risk of visible staining.
2. **Double Wash**: Because you are using oil and turmeric, a single shampoo might not be enough. Always do a 'Double Wash' to ensure no yellow residue is left on the scalp.
3. **Consistency**: Like any natural remedy, this works best when done consistently. Try it once a week for four weeks for a complete 'Scalp Reset'.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can turmeric cause hair loss?**
A: No. In fact, by reducing scalp inflammation and fungal growth, turmeric creates a healthier environment for hair growth. Some studies even suggest curcumin may help inhibit DHT (a hormone linked to hair loss) when used topically.

**Q: Is it okay to use on a greasy scalp?**
A: Yes. While it seems counterintuitive to add oil to a greasy scalp, coconut oil helps dissolve the 'bad' oxidized oils on your scalp, and the lemon juice helps regulate sebum production.

**Q: What if it burns?**
A: A slight tingling from the lemon juice is normal. However, if you feel a burning sensation, wash it off immediately. You may have a sensitivity to lemon or a cut on your scalp.
