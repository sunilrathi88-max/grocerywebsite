# Coriander Jeera Aloo: 15-Minute Sabzi Every Beginner Can Nail

Jeera aloo with dhaniya powder is India's most forgiving recipe — no onion, no garlic, no blending, 15 minutes, and reliably delicious every time. This version adds the Rajasthani **Amchur** finish that lifts the whole dish from simple to spectacular.

## The 3-Spice Magic

The beauty of Jeera Aloo lies in three staples: Whole Cumin (Jeera), [Rathi Naturals Salem Turmeric](/products/turmeric-powder), and a generous amount of [Ramganj Mandi Dhaniya Powder](/products/coriander-powder).

While the cumin provides the 'crunch' and the turmeric provides the 'color', the coriander powder is what provides the 'coat'. It creates a beautiful, crusty masala layer around each cube of potato that keeps it from being plain and dry.

## The Technique: The 'Crusty' Aloo

1. Boil potatoes until 90% cooked. Peel and cube.
2. Heat oil/ghee. Add jeera and let it crackle.
3. Add the potatoes and sauté until the edges turn golden brown.
4. **The Critical Part**: Sprinkle dhaniya powder, turmeric, and red chilli powder over the golden potatoes. Sauté on low heat for 2 minutes. The powder will stick to the crispy potato edges, creating a flavorful crust.
5. Finish with a squeeze of lime or a pinch of Amchur (Dry Mango Powder).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use raw potatoes?**
A: Yes, but it will take 15-20 minutes longer. Steaming or pressure-cooking them beforehand ensures they stay 'extra crispy' on the outside and soft inside.

**Q: My masala is burning, what should I do?**
A: Turn the heat to 'Low' before adding the dry powders. If it still looks dry, add a teaspoon of water—it will create a steam that helps the masala stick to the potato without burning.
