## Recipe Deep-Dive: Technique, Ratios and Common Mistakes

The specific ratios and technique for this recipe have been developed through decades of Indian cooking tradition. The standard approach — adding garam masala powder at the wrong phase of cooking — is the most common error that prevents home cooks from achieving professional results.

The correct method positions garam masala powder at the phase in the recipe where its aromatic compounds can bloom without being destroyed: in hot fat at the beginning for foundational flavour, or in the last minutes of cooking for aromatic finishing. Never add cold-ground garam masala powder to boiling water directly without a fat phase intermediary. The fat extracts the oil-soluble aromatic compounds first, then they distribute evenly through the entire dish as water is added.

> [!TIP]
> **The Finishing Touch**
> Garam Masala is a "warm" spice blend, and it's best used as a finisher. Adding a pinch in the last 2 minutes of cooking allows the heat of the dish to release the aromas without "cooking them out." This is how you get that restaurant-style fragrance that greets you at the table.
