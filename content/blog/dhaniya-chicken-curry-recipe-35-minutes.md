# Easy Dhaniya Chicken Curry: Fragrant, Homestyle, Ready in 35 Minutes

Dhaniya chicken curry uses coriander as the backbone of the masala rather than a finishing spice. At **3 tablespoons of [Dhaniya powder](/products/coriander-powder) per 500g chicken**, the curry becomes distinctly fragrant, earthy and complex — without the heavy heat or richness of more elaborate restaurant preparations.

## The 'Golden Ratio' for Chicken

Most chicken curries rely on Onion-Tomato masala. This version relies on the Spice-Curd emulsion. By whisking a large amount of [Rathi Naturals Ramganj Mandi dhaniya powder](/products/coriander-powder) into thick dahi (yogurt) before adding it to the chicken, you create a velvety, non-greasy sauce that is impossibly aromatic.

## The 35-Minute Method

1. **Marinate**: Toss 500g chicken with 1 teaspoon turmeric, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste.
2. **The Base**: Sauté 2 sliced onions until translucent. Do not brown them—we want a 'pale' sweet base to highlight the green coriander.
3. **The Masala**: In a bowl, mix 3 tablespoons [Rathi Naturals Dhaniya Powder](/products/coriander-powder) with 1 cup thick curd. Whisk until smooth.
4. **Cook**: Add the chicken to the onions. Sauté for 5 minutes. Lower the heat and add the curd-dhaniya mixture.
5. **Slow-Simmer**: Cover and cook for 20 minutes. The coriander will absorb the chicken's juices, expanding and thickening into a lush gravy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use this for mutton instead?**
A: Yes, but mutton takes longer to cook. Use a pressure cooker and add an extra tablespoon of [Dhaniya powder](/products/coriander-powder) to stand up to the stronger meat flavor.

**Q: My curd always splits. How do I prevent this?**
A: Whisk the spices _into_ the curd before adding. Most importantly, lower the heat to 'Simmer' before adding the curd to the hot pan.
