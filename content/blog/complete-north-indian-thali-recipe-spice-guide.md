## Recipe Deep-Dive: Technique, Ratios and Common Mistakes

The specific ratios and technique for this recipe have been developed through decades of Indian cooking tradition. The standard approach — adding dhaniya powder at the wrong phase of cooking — is the most common error that prevents home cooks from achieving professional results. The correct method positions dhaniya powder at the phase in the recipe where its aromatic compounds can bloom without being destroyed: in hot fat at the beginning for foundational flavour, or in the last minutes of cooking for aromatic finishing. Never add cold-ground spices powder to boiling water directly without a fat phase intermediary. The fat extracts the oil-soluble aromatic compounds first, then they distribute evenly through the entire dish as water is added. This principle applies across Indian cooking: every spice works better when bloomed in oil or ghee first.

> [!TIP]
> **The Layering Technique**
> When cooking multiple dishes for a thali, layer your spices. Add the earthy Coriander and Turmeric early in the process to build a base, and save the aromatic Garam Masala for the final finish. This ensures each dish in your thali has a distinct, layered profile.
