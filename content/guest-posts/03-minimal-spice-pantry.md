## The Essential 7

These seven spices cover the full spectrum of flavor: Earthy, Spicy, Sweet, Pungent, Bitter, and Aromatic.

1.  **Turmeric Powder (Haldi):** The healer. Provides the base color and earthy foundation.
2.  **Red Chilli Powder (Lal Mirch):** The heat. Essential for kick and vibrant red color.
3.  **Coriander Powder (Dhaniya):** The body. A thickener that adds citrusy, woodsy notes and volume to gravies.
4.  **Cumin Seeds (Jeera):** The nutty aroma. Used whole in tempering to start a dish.
5.  **Mustard Seeds (Rai) OR Fenugreek Seeds (Methi):** _Choose based on region._ North Indian? Go with Fenugreek. South/West Indian? Mustard is non-negotiable. Let's include **Fenugreek** for versatility in veg dishes and batters.
6.  **Black Pepper (Kali Mirch):** The sharp heat. Distinct from chili, it adds upper-nasal heat and warmth.
7.  **Garam Masala:** The finisher. A complex blend of aromatic spices (cloves, cardamom, cinnamon) used at the _end_ of cooking.

## The Magic Formulas: What You Can Cook

You might think 7 spices are limiting. They aren't. It's all about the _combination_ and _timing_.

### 1. The "Tadka" Trinity (Tempering)

_Cumin + Turmeric + Red Chilli_
**Dishes:** Jeera Aloo, Yellow Dal Fry, simple sautéed vegetables (Beans, Cabbage).
**Method:** Heat oil -> Sputter Cumin -> Add Veggies -> Add Turmeric/Chilli -> Salt. Done.

### 2. The Curry Base

_Cumin (whole) + Coriander (powder) + Turmeric + Chilli + Garam Masala_
**Dishes:** Chicken Curry, Aloo Gobi, Matar Paneer, Rajma.
**Method:** Sauté onions/ginger/garlic -> Add Turmeric/Chilli/Coriander -> Fry until oil separates -> Add main ingredient -> Finish with Garam Masala.

### 3. The Digestive Blend

_Cumin + Fenugreek + Black Pepper_
**Dishes:** Kadhi, Pumpkin Sabzi, Buttermilk (Chaas) seasoning.
**Method:** Fenugreek adds a maple-like bitterness that cuts through rich yogurt gravies (Kadhi) or sweet vegetables (Pumpkin).

### 4. The Aromatics

_Garam Masala + Black Pepper + Turmeric_
**Dishes:** Masala Chai, Egg Roast, Pepper Chicken.
**Method:** Here, Black Pepper takes center stage for heat, while Garam Masala provides the floral notes.

## Storage: Keeping the 7 Fresh

Since you're buying fewer spices, treat them better.

1.  **Air is the Enemy:** Store in airtight glass jars, not the plastic bags they came in.
2.  **Darkness is Friend:** UV light degrades color (especially Turmeric and Chilli). Keep them in a drawer or cupboard, not on the windowsill.
3.  **Heat Kills:** Don't store spices directly above your stove. The rising heat and steam will cause clumping and flavor loss.
