## GUEST POST #3: Building a Minimal Spice Pantry

**Target Publication:** My Weekend Kitchen (minimalism + cooking angle)

**Post Title:** "The Minimalist Spice Pantry: 10 Spices That Do 90% of Your Cooking"

**Meta Description:** "Tired of 50+ spices gathering dust? Build a focused, minimal spice collection that handles 90% of your cooking."

**Word Count:** 1,500-1,800 words

**Author Bio:**

> Rathi Naturals. believes in intentional spice selection. Fresh, tested, kept-in-rotation spices beat a stockpile of expired bottles.

### OUTLINE

**INTRODUCTION**

- Hook: "Most home cooks have 40-50 spices. 30 are expired."
- Problem: Spice hoarding → waste & stale flavor
- Solution: Build a minimal, rotational spice pantry
- Outcome: Better flavor + lower costs + less waste

**THE PHILOSOPHY**

- Minimalism applied to spices
- Rotation vs collection
- Quality over quantity
- Knowing your ingredients

**THE 10-SPICE SYSTEM**

**1. CUMIN (Jeera) - 2/5 stars utility**

- Why: Base of 50%+ Indian curries
- Forms: Whole + powder
- Dishes: Dals, curries, rice, breads

**2. CORIANDER (Dhania) - 2/5 stars utility**

- Why: Complements cumin perfectly
- Forms: Whole + powder
- Dishes: Curries, chutneys, spice blends

**3. TURMERIC (Haldi) - 1/5 stars utility**

- Why: Essential, medicinal, unique
- Forms: Powder only
- Dishes: Almost all savory dishes

**4. RED CHILLI POWDER (Mirch) - 1.5/5 stars utility**

- Why: Heat control in all dishes
- Forms: Powder only
- Caution: Buy fresh, store in fridge

**5. GINGER (Adrak) - 1.5/5 stars utility**

- Why: Fresh + ground uses
- Forms: Fresh root + powder
- Dishes: Curries, teas, ginger-garlic paste

**6. GARLIC - 1.5/5 stars utility**

- Why: Ginger's partner
- Forms: Fresh + powder (optional)
- Dishes: Base of most savory cooking

**7. GARAM MASALA - 1.5/5 stars utility**

- Why: Eliminates need for 5+ spices
- Forms: Powder only
- Dishes: Finishing spice for curries

**8. CARDAMOM - 1/5 stars utility**

- Why: Rare but essential (teas, biryanis, sweets)
- Forms: Whole pods preferred
- Dishes: Chai, biryanis, desserts

**9. CINNAMON (Dalchini) - 0.5/5 stars utility**

- Why: Specific uses but transforms dishes
- Forms: Sticks or powder
- Dishes: Teas, biryanis, biryani, rice dishes

**10. BAY LEAF (Tej Patta) - 0.5/5 stars utility**

- Why: Subtle but irreplaceable in rice, dals
- Forms: Whole leaves only
- Dishes: Rice cooking, dals, slow-cooked curries

**OPTIONAL EXTRAS (when you want to expand)**

- Black pepper (grinder + freshly ground)
- Cloves (rare uses but impactful)
- Fenugreek leaves (fresh or dried)
- Mustard seeds (for tadka)

**BUILDING YOUR PANTRY (Actionable Steps)**

1. Buy whole spices (fresher, longer shelf life)
2. Store in airtight containers in cool place
3. Label with purchase date
4. Rotate: Use oldest first
5. Replace when flavor fades
