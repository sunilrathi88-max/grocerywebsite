# Link Building Outreach Kit (Week 2)

Use these templates to conduct your cold outreach. They are designed to be personal, value-first, and low-friction.

**Golden Rules:**

1.  **Customize the first sentence.** Prove you read their blog.
2.  **Don't ask for a link immediately** (except in the Calculator pitch). Ask for _interest_.
3.  **Follow up once.** 50% of replies come from the follow-up.

---

## Template A: The "Guest Post" Pitch

**Goal:** Get your "Regional Spice Blends" or "Ayurvedic Science" article published on their site.
**Target:** Food blogs that accept contributors (e.g., IndianFoodRecipesOnline, regional food blogs).

**Subject:** Idea for [Blog Name] readers: "Forgotten Regional Masalas"
**Alt Subject:** Guest contribution: 5 Regional Blends (w/ recipes)

**Body:**

```text
Hi [Name],

I've been cooking from your [Specific Recipe Name] recipe for months—your tip about [specific detail from their post] was a game changer for me.

I noticed you often write about authentic regional flavors, but I haven't seen much coverage on the specific regional spice blends outside of just "Garam Masala."

I've drafted a comprehensive guide titled:
**"5 Forgotten Regional Spice Blends That Deserve a Comeback in 2025"**

It covers blends like Chettinad Masala (with Stone Flower) and Kashmiri Ver, including the history and a DIY recipe for each. It’s strictly educational, not promotional.

Would you be open to taking a look at the draft? If it's a fit for [Blog Name], I'd love to contribute it to your readers.

Best,
[Your Name]
Founder, Rathi Naturals.
```

## Template B: The "Calculator Resource" Pitch

**Goal:** Get them to link to your Spice Freshness Calculator as a "resource" in their existing articles (e.g., "How to store spices").
**Target:** Blogs with articles like "Pantry Organization," "Spice Guide," or "Cooking Tips."

**Subject:** Quick resource for your "Spice Storage" guide
**Alt Subject:** Free tool to check spice expiration

**Body:**

```text
Hi [Name],

I was reading your guide on [Name of their article, e.g., How to ORganize Your Pantry] and loved the section on keeping ingredients fresh.

I actually built a free tool recently that might be useful for your readers who struggle with this. It’s a **Spice Freshness Calculator**—it uses an algorithm (based on storage method + sensory check) to tell you if a spice is still potent or needs replacing.

Here it is: [Link to Calculator]

It’s completely free/no-ads. If you think it adds value, feel free to add it as a resource to your guide.

Either way, thanks for the great content!

Cheers,
[Your Name]
```

## Template C: The "Affiliate / Review" Pitch

**Goal:** Recruit them to the Affiliate Program or get a product review.
**Target:** Influencers/Bloggers who do product reviews (Instagram/YouTube focus).

**Subject:** Paid Collab: Sourcing authentic spices for [Blog Name]
**Alt Subject:** Sending you some samples (Rathi Naturals.)

**Body:**

```text
Hi [Name],

Big fan of your [Platform] channel. Your recent video on [Topic] was fantastic.

I'm the founder of **Rathi Naturals.**, a new spice brand focusing on lab-tested purity and farm-direct sourcing from Rajasthan and Kerala. We're trying to solve the "adulterated spice" problem in India.

I'd love to send you a **free "Essentials Box"** (our Turmeric, 8mm Cardamom, and Lakadong Chilli) for you to try in your kitchen. No strings attached—I just want real cooks to taste the difference.

If you love them, we also have an affiliate partner program (15% commission) if you ever wanted to share them with your audience.

Let me know if I can ship a box to you?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Link to Website]
```

## The "Polite Nudge" (Follow-up)

**Send:** 3-4 days after the first email if no reply.

**Subject:** Re: [Original Subject]

**Body:**

```text
Hi [Name],

Just bubbling this up in case it got buried.

Let me know if you're interested in the [Guest Post / Samples / Tool]—no pressure at all if you're too busy right now!

Best,
[Your Name]
```
